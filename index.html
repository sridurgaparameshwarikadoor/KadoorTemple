Sri Durga Parameshwari Temple, Padukaduru

About the Temple

Sri Durga Parameshwari Temple, Padukaduru, is one of the most divine and ancient temples located amidst the lush greenery near Mandarthi, about 6 km away. The powerful Goddess Durga Mata has been blessing her devotees for generations, protecting them and guiding them through their lives. The serene surroundings and devotional atmosphere attract devotees throughout the year.

Major Poojas & Festivals

Bhajana mangolostava (January 8 – 15)

Bhajana mangolostava begins on January 8th with devotional bhajans every night. On January 14th, bhajans start early in the morning and continue through the night until dawn of January 15th. The celebrations conclude with a grand Satyanarayana Puja on January 15th.

Annual Festival (April 30)

The Annual Festival is celebrated with great devotion every year on April 30th, featuring Kenda Seve, Bevu Seve, Kola Seve, and Tulabhara Seve.

Sone Aarati Seve

In the auspicious month of Sone Maasa, special pujas are conducted every evening for all 30 days. Devotees gather in the temple to offer prayers and participate in bhajans and other devotional activities.

Namura Hostu / Kadiru Kattuva Habba

On this day, the temple distributes Kadiru to devotees, enabling them to celebrate Hostu rituals in their own homes as part of the temple’s blessings.

Navaratri Utsava

Celebrated for all 10 days of Navaratri with devotion and grandeur. On the 10th day (Vijayadashami), a special Dashami Puja is performed.

Deepostava

Celebrated in the month of Kartika, Deepostava marks the festival of lights and devotion, symbolizing the triumph of light over darkness.

Daily Poojas

The temple conducts daily Poojas along with Hoovina Aarati and Maha Mangala Aarati with deep devotion and love for Goddess Durga Mata.
